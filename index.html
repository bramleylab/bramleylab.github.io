Bramley Lab

Computational Cognitive Science Group at the University of Edinburgh

About

Bramley Lab is a brand new computational cognitive science focused lab, based in the Psychology department at the University of Edinburgh and affiliated with the Informatics Forum. We welcome prospective PhD students, PostDocs and study participants.

Mission

We are interested in understanding how human minds learn about the world, how they represent and use what they learn. In particular, we focus on active learning (how people gather information from the world), causality (how they represent the world’s dynamics), discovery (how people generate new ideas, concepts and theories), and control (how people utilise their causal knowledge to achieve their goals).

Approach

We take a computational approach to studying human cognition. We sometimes collect behavioural data by running experiments in the lab, but often collect large behavioural datasets online using psiTurk and websites like Amazon mechanical Turk and Prolific Academic. We build and compare computational models and simulations against human data to reverse engineer human intelligence.
